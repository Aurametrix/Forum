What is happening in my body when I am running out of patience?

posted Sep 20, 2015 by Wanda Cohen
Great explanation on reddit, but it's only about the brain.
Imagine the part of your brain that gets irritated and annoyed is your thumb (it's called the amygdala). Now imagine the part of your brain that has to be reasonable and calm is the rest of your fingers (this is the frontal lobe and surrounding grey matter). Wrap your fingers around your thumb. This is pretty much your brains natural resting state, the fingers keep the thumb in line. Now imagine that something just keeps bothering you and that thumb keeps bouncing around. At a certain point the rest of your fingers get tired and then the thumb is in control. You've just run out of patience.

What is happening in the body?

Different areas of the brain help us to distinguish emotional states and be patient or impatient, egoistic and unsympathetic or empathetic and compassionate. Amygdala hijacks the rational brain by secreting molecules such as vasopressin and corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH), these molecules in their turn stimulate the secretion of specific hormones or neurotransmitters.
Signalling molecules can give you a burst of energy, accelerate your heart rate, rise your blood pressure and your rate of breathing, make your face flush because of increased blood flow. This will make your attention to narrow and you to focus only on your emotions.
