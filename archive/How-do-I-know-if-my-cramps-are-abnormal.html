Yes, pain is subjective, but if your cramps are interfering with your life, it's worth checking in with your doctor. Why suffer?
Aurametrix data proved that abdominal pain such as from IBS or menstrual periods can be reduced with better diet and exercise. Study participants self-reported the severity of pain and observing their behavior. But measuring pain objectively (and inexpensively!) might be soon possible.

fMRI can be used to objectively measure physical pain

Cheaper and portable version of a "pain detector" could use near-infrared spectroscopy and is currently being developed.

Cramps are physical muscle contractions and can be measured by electrical activity or pressure-based methods evaluating stiffness (muscle tension), ability to relax, and contractions. At the hospital, invasive internal monitoring is often used like tocodynamometer that measures the force of uterine contractionsin pregnancy, intra uterine pressure catheter,

Just a few tips on how to ease abdominal (uterine) cramps:

1. Stretch with Yoga. Some of the suggestions to prevent and reduce muscle cramps work in this case too. Poses targeting the pelvis and lumbar region, where period pain is the worst, may be the most helpful.
2. Place a heating pad or hot water bottle on your lower belly, just below your belly button
3. Indulge yourself with a massage and aromatherapy
4. Drink red raspberry leaf tea
