Is Coffee Good or Bad For You?

Aurametrix users observe a range of coffee drinking effects: from boosting feeling of optimizm and sports performance to dehydration, body odor and bad breath. Of course, it depends on when we drink it, how much was drunk, our genes and microbes, and other things we ate and did.

It's about timing: people that drink coffee after lunch seem to be more content and more interested in their work. Yet drinking coffee after 2pm leads to worse sleep.  

It's about microbes that call us home  -  how many of each kind we have in our mouth, intestines and on the skin. Scientific studies showed that coffee drinking increases activity and numbers of Bifidobacteria - the kind of bacteria that lives in fermented milk and yogurt and is supposed to be good for you. 

It's about genes - cytochrome P450 in the liver determines if we are fast or slow metabolizers. 

It's about everything else. Coffee taken with bad news and world anxiety, instead of cream and sugar is a lethal concoction, says Leila. Coffee with no food is no good, say participants of this anxiety forum. Coffee after fast food is another no no. 

What about you? Any interesting observations or combinations?

According to a 20+ year AARP study, healthy people who drank three or more cups of coffee per day and didn't smoke had a 10% change of living longer than those who were abstaining from coffee. If you don't have problems metabolizing it, coffee seems to protect against heart disease, respiratory disease, stroke, injuries and accidents, diabetes, and infections, but not cancer.

A study published in this March 2012 article showed that caffeine improves language processing if taken at 200 mg (for those who do not drink much coffee) or 400 mg (for heavy drinkers). 

Women who drink a cup or more of coffee daily have up to a 25% lower stroke risk than those who did not, according to another study reported this month in the journal Stroke.

In various other studies coffee was shown to protect people from type 2 diabetes - probably because of its magnesium and chromium content, and help mice avoid skin cancer (if they were also vigorously exercising). Just smelling coffee seems to protect from stress and cavities. It also seemed to help Scandinavians drinking unfiltered Scandinavian types of coffee (similar to French press, Turkish or Greek varieties). Yet, drinking too much coffee can cause insomnia,  digestive issues, a racing heart, high blood pressure, nervousness, irritability, and nausea.

Here's one more: Consumption of 3 or more cups of caffeinated coffee increases the risk of developing glaucoma And the newest article: caffeine in nectar boosts bee's memories.
